This is THE Batman movie that fans have been waiting to see. I caught an advance screening a few weeks before the release, and am happy to report that they didn’t screw this one up. Christian Bale stars as Bruce Wayne, coming to grips with tragedy that befalls him by searching for life’s meaning. It’s a quest that will eventually lead him to become the masked vigilante Batman, who hides in the shadows dispelling his brand of justice. As this is the origin of Batman, there is a lot of setup, or in other words: There is a lot of story. It’s not a slam bang, no time for air action film. It takes it’s time allowing the viewer to understand the point of Wayne, why he would do what he does. A great supporting cast, of course, always helps and here we have Liam Neeson as Wayne’s mentor Ducard, Morgan Freeman as Lucias Fox, a man who can just about invent anything, Michael Cain as trusted butler Alfred, and Cillian Murphy who absolutely steals the movie as Dr. Jonathan Crane. Props go to Christopher Nolan who has created a very believable Gotham City and a new look Batmobile which embarks on one of the best car chases ever captured on film. A little warning to parents: There are some very scary scenes throughout, so be advised. For everyone else, a great time to be had!!
Batman Begins. Opening Night. IMAX! Forget any of the previous Batman movies exist. Even without the IMAX extras it's a great film, even better than Spidey 1 & 2, both of which I enjoyed. Christian Bale has the right body, voice, disarming charm and brooding manner to play the part perfectly. I also liked that Gary Oldman plays a good guy this time around. Maybe it's just me, but I find Katie Holmes' crooked smile distracting (maybe it was the IMAX screen size) and she reminded me too much of Kirsten Dunst in the Spidey movies, alternating between fiercely independent and swooningly incapable. The sets, stunts and special effects are tremendous, and there were also many laugh out loud moments. It's always a fun night at the movies when a good director, great cast and crisp script come together to remind me why I enjoy plunking down $14.00 to sit shoulder to shoulder with 200 other people. Highly recommended!
